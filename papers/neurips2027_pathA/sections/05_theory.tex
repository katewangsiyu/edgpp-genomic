This section states HCCP's three hero theorems: \textbf{Theorem~\ref{thm:t3}} (T3, exact bin-conditional coverage) and the principal new contribution \textbf{Theorems~\ref{thm:t5oracle}--\ref{thm:t5lower}} (T5.1 oracle bin count + T5.2 matching minimax lower bound). Auxiliary lemmas (T1/T2 corollaries at coarser partitions; T1$'$/T2$'$/T3$'$/T4 robust counterparts via \citet{barber2023exchangeability} Theorem~2; T3-loc feature-ball extension; T3.b $\shat$-perturbation robustness) are stated and proved in App.~\ref{app:proofs}.

Unless otherwise noted, all statements take the calibration fold $\D^{(-c^\star)}$ and the test point $(X, Y) \sim P_{c^\star}$ as in §\ref{sec:formulation:setup}, and $\pset$ is the Mondrian prediction set of Eq.~\eqref{eq:pset}. We use the A1/A1$'$/A2 hierarchy of \ref{ass:A1}--\ref{ass:A2}; two further assumptions used only by appendix lemmas (\textbf{A3-loc}, \textbf{B1}) are stated explicitly in App.~\ref{app:proofs}.

\subsection{Theorem 1: Bin-conditional coverage}
\label{sec:theory:t3}

\begin{theorem}[T3 bin-conditional coverage, exact]
\label{thm:t3}
Under \ref{ass:A1prime} and A2-cell (A2 at the $(k, b)$-cell level), for each cell with $n_{k,b} \geq \lceil 1/\alpha \rceil$ and continuous $F_{kb}$,
\[
1 - \alpha \;\leq\; P\bigl(Y \in \pset(X) \mid Y = k,\, b(X) = b\bigr) \;\leq\; 1 - \alpha + \frac{1}{n_{k,b} + 1}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\paragraph{Proof sketch.}
The standard split-CP rank-uniformity argument carried inside a single Mondrian cell: under A1$'$~+~A2-cell, the calibration scores restricted to cell $(k, b)$ are i.i.d.\ $F_{kb}$, the test score is independent and $F_{kb}$-distributed, and exchangeability of the augmented sequence yields the two-sided rate $1/(n_{k,b}+1)$ via Lemma~\ref{lem:cp_quantile} (full proof in App.~\ref{app:proofs:t3}).

\paragraph{Positioning against \citet{barber2020limits}.}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=gray!5!white, colframe=gray!60!black, arc=1mm, boxrule=0.4pt]
\textbf{We do not claim pointwise conditional coverage} $P(Y \in \pset(X) \mid X = x) \geq 1 - \alpha$, which \citet{barber2020limits} rule out in finite samples absent structural assumptions. Theorem~\ref{thm:t3} guarantees coverage conditional on the partition event $b(X) = b$ and label $Y = k$. Our $\shat$-bins are the \emph{coarsest practically useful} strengthening of standard class-conditional CP that still carries finite-sample bounds: any refinement (e.g., $\shat$-quantile or feature-ball conditioning) must either invoke an additional regularity assumption (as T3-loc does via A3-loc) or accept a slower $O(n^{-2/(d+2)})$ rate (as RLCP does). HCCP deliberately stops at the $(y, b)$-product partition where T3 applies.
\end{tcolorbox}

\paragraph{Auxiliary lemmas in App.~\ref{app:proofs}.}
Theorem~\ref{thm:t3} specialises to standard split-CP at coarser partitions: marginalising $b$ recovers \emph{class-conditional} coverage (Lem.~\ref{thm:t2}), and further marginalising $y$ recovers \emph{marginal} coverage (Lem.~\ref{thm:t1}). When A2 fails but \ref{ass:A1} holds, T1, T2, and T3 degrade gracefully into robust forms T1$'$/T2$'$/T3$'$ via the underlying weighted-swap bound of \citet{barber2023exchangeability} Theorem~2 with uniform calibration weights (Lem.~\ref{thm:t4}); the cell-level instance is Lem.~\ref{thm:t3prime}. Two further extensions are appendix-only: \emph{feature-ball coverage} T3-loc, which translates partition-level T3 to coverage on $\ell_2$-balls under $L$-Lipschitz $\shat$ + A3-loc (Lem.~\ref{thm:t3loc}), and \emph{$\shat$-plug-in robustness} T3.b, which establishes graceful degradation when the variance head is misspecified by relative error $\eta$ (Lem.~\ref{thm:t3b}).

\paragraph{T3 vs T3$'$ on TraitGym.}
T3$'$ degrades T3 by exactly the cell-level chrom-TV $\bar\delta_{\mathrm{TV}}^{(k,b)}$ that our per-chromosome KS audit (App.~\ref{app:data:ks}) bounds empirically. On Complex the audit is close to the $5\%$ null ($10.4\%$ rejection at $p < 0.05$, max KS $0.315$), so T3 exact is adequate. On Mendelian the audit rejects $46\%$ of cell-chrom pairs, driven by the highest-$\shat$ positive-class cell where pathogenic variants concentrate on a few chromosomes (chr9, chr7, chr3); $d_{\mathrm{TV}} \leq \mathrm{KS}/\sqrt{2}$ then gives $\bar\delta_{\mathrm{TV}}^{(k,b)} \leq 0.31$ on the worst cell, consistent with the observed per-chromosome coverage gap $\leq 0.073$ (Table~\ref{tab:perchrom}). T3$'$ is therefore the operational guarantee for Mendelian; T3 exact remains valid on Complex.

\subsection{Theorems 2--3: Adaptive bin count and matching minimax lower bound}
\label{sec:theory:t5}

\textbf{This is the principal theoretical contribution.} Theorem~\ref{thm:t3} holds for any fixed $K$ but says nothing about \emph{which} $K$ to choose. We now derive the optimal $K$ by formalising the bias--variance tradeoff that governs bin-conditional coverage and prove a matching minimax lower bound to establish that the rate is tight, dimension-free, and not improvable without extra assumptions.

\paragraph{Two sources of coverage gap.}
Under \ref{ass:ASL}, the worst-cell coverage gap decomposes as
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:gap_decomp}
G(K) \;:=\; \max_{k,b} \bigl|\mathrm{cov}_{k,b} - (1 - \alpha)\bigr| \;\leq\; \underbrace{\frac{L_F \cdot R}{K}}_{\text{stationarity bias}} \;+\; \underbrace{\frac{K}{\pi_{\min} \cdot n}}_{\text{finite-sample variance}},
\end{equation}
where $R = \shat_{\max} - \shat_{\min}$ is the $\shat$ range and $\pi_{\min} = \min(\pi, 1 - \pi)$ the minority-class prevalence. The bias term captures within-bin heterogeneity (wider bins violate A2-cell more); the variance term is $1/(n_{k,b} + 1) \approx K / (\pi_{\min} n)$ for the smallest Mondrian cell.

\begin{theorem}[T5.1 Oracle bin count]
\label{thm:t5oracle}
Under \ref{ass:A1prime} and \ref{ass:ASL} with equiprobable $\shat$-bins,
\[
K^\star \;=\; \bigl\lfloor \sqrt{L_F \cdot R \cdot \pi_{\min} \cdot n}\,\bigr\rfloor, \qquad G(K^\star) \;\leq\; \frac{2\sqrt{L_F \cdot R}}{\sqrt{\pi_{\min} \cdot n}}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[T5.2 Matching lower bound]
\label{thm:t5lower}
For any Mondrian conformal predictor using equiprobable $\shat$-bins, there exists a distribution satisfying \ref{ass:A1prime} and \ref{ass:ASL} such that $\min_K G(K) = \Omega\!\bigl(\sqrt{L_F R / (\pi_{\min} n)}\bigr)$, so the $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate is tight.
\end{theorem}

\paragraph{Proof sketch.}
For T5.1, set $g'(K) = -L_F R / K^2 + 1/(\pi_{\min} n) = 0$ to obtain $K^\star = \sqrt{L_F R \pi_{\min} n}$; substitution gives $g(K^\star) = 2\sqrt{L_F R / (\pi_{\min} n)}$, with the floor introducing $O(1/(\pi_{\min} n))$ slack dominated by the leading term. For T5.2, construct a linear-shift family $F_{k,\sigma}(s) = \Phi(s - L_F \sigma)$; both the bias and variance lower bounds are tight on this family. Full proofs in App.~\ref{app:proofs:t5}.

\paragraph{Dimension-free rate --- what it does and does not say.}
The $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate of Theorem~\ref{thm:t5oracle} is \emph{dimension-free} in the sense that the constant does not depend on the feature dimension $d$; it does depend on $L_F \cdot R$, the Lipschitz constant of the conditional score CDF with respect to the 1-D summary $\shat(x)$, and on the minority-class prevalence $\pi_{\min}$. This is a different guarantee from RLCP's \citep{hore2025rlcp} feature-space local coverage rate $O(n^{-2/(d+2)})$: RLCP targets $P(Y \in \pset(X) \mid X \approx x)$, our T3/T5 target $P(Y \in \pset(X) \mid \shat(X) \in B_b)$. We do not claim the two rates are comparable on their native targets; we claim that the $\shat$-bin conditioning is the coarsest practically useful conditioning under which a dimension-free rate exists, and T5 quantifies the exact constant $L_F \cdot R$ it costs. At $d = 7{,}731$ (our feature dimension), RLCP's rate is intractable on any realistic calibration set size, so the two methods occupy different operating points rather than competing on the same metric; T3-loc (Lem.~\ref{thm:t3loc}) provides the feature-ball bridge at the cost of the additional A3-loc assumption.

\paragraph{Comparison to SC-CP's $O(n^{-2/3})$ rate.}
\citet{vanderlaan2024sccp} achieve a faster $O(n^{-2/3})$ rate (their Thm.~4.2) on the $\hat\mu$-axis via the monotone structure of isotonic regression, inheriting the segment-count concentration of \citet{deng2021isotonic} \emph{without} requiring \ref{ass:ASL}. Our $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate trades a slower asymptotic rate for three properties SC-CP does not provide: (i) conditioning on $\shat$ rather than $\hat\mu$, which adapts to mean-independent heteroscedasticity (SC-CP §5 explicitly notes its $\hat\mu$-axis binning cannot adapt unless heteroscedasticity is mean-driven); (ii) explicit dependence on minority-class prevalence $\pi_{\min}$, which makes T5 directly applicable to severely imbalanced classification (TraitGym $\pi_{\min} \approx 0.10$) where SC-CP's regression formulation does not encode the class-conditional sample-size constraint; (iii) a matching minimax lower bound (Thm.~\ref{thm:t5lower}) under \ref{ass:ASL}, certifying that the $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate is unimprovable in the $(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ regime without strengthening assumptions. The two results are therefore complementary rather than competing: SC-CP is the recommended choice when heteroscedasticity is mean-driven; T5 is the recommended choice when $\shat$ is supplied as an independent learned summary, as in DEGU-style \citep{zhou2026degu} VEP pipelines.

\paragraph{Practical $\hat K$ selection.}
We select $\hat K$ via cross-validation: for each candidate $K \in \{2, 3, 5, 8, 10, 15, 20\}$, we compute the leave-one-out worst-cell coverage gap on the calibration fold and pick $\hat K_{\mathrm{CV}} = \operatorname{argmin}_K \hat G(K)$. On TraitGym, $\hat K_{\mathrm{CV}} = 3$ (Mendelian) and $\hat K_{\mathrm{CV}} = 2$ (Complex), both below the $K = 5$ default, with $6{-}7\times$ worst-cell gap improvement (§\ref{sec:experiments}). The low optimal $K$ reflects class imbalance ($\pi_{\min} \approx 0.10$): with few positive variants, the variance term $K / (\pi_{\min} n)$ dominates.

\paragraph{Asymptotic vs.\ finite-sample regimes.}
A reader may notice the gap between asymptotic $K^\star$ and the cross-validated $\hat K_{\mathrm{CV}}$. Plugging the LCLS estimate of $L_F$ \citep{lcls2024lipschitz} into Theorem~\ref{thm:t5oracle} gives $K^\star = 22$ (Mendelian, $\hat L_F = 2.97 \in [2.89, 3.45]$) and $K^\star = 50$ (Complex, $\hat L_F = 4.51 \in [4.47, 4.69]$); see App.~\ref{app:tables:ksweep}, Table~\ref{tab:LF_estimators} for the bootstrap CI. These are still above $\hat K_{\mathrm{CV}} \in \{2, 3\}$, but the legacy max-of-ratios estimator overstates $K^\star$ by an order of magnitude ($88$ Mendelian, $110$ Complex), so the apparent contradiction is largely an artefact of the estimator. The remaining gap is genuine: T5.1 characterises the \emph{asymptotic} optimum where Eq.~\ref{eq:gap_decomp} is binding, while in the finite-sample regime ($n \leq 11{,}400$) the per-cell sample-size floor $n_{k,b} \geq \lceil 1/\alpha \rceil$ becomes binding before the variance term reaches the bias term. The empirical $K$-sweep extended to $K = 200$ (App.~\ref{app:tables:ksweep}) exposes a third \emph{fallback-dominated} regime not modelled by Eq.~\ref{eq:gap_decomp}, in which the predictor reverts to T2 pooled class-conditional thresholds and the worst-cell gap saturates. We therefore recommend $\hat K_{\mathrm{CV}}$ as the practical selector at the sample sizes typical of human-variant benchmarks; the gap to the LCLS-derived $K^\star$ closes as $n \to \infty$.

\paragraph{Validation map.}
§\ref{sec:experiments} validates each hero theorem: \textbf{T3} on trait-LOO (the bin-local gap reaches the finite-sample floor $0.002$–$0.004$, matching the $1/(n_{k,b}+1)$ rate) and on chrom-LOO with the SOTA feature set (gap $\leq 0.077$); \textbf{T5} via the K-sweep ($\hat K_{\mathrm{CV}}$ yields $6$–$7\times$ tighter worst-cell gap than $K = 5$). T3$'$ absorbs the cross-dataset M$\to$C excess via the marginal-score TV proxy.
