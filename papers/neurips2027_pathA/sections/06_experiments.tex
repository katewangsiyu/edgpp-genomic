\subsection{Setup}

\paragraph{Datasets.} TraitGym Mendelian matched\_9 ($n = 3380$, 113 OMIM) and Complex matched\_9 ($n = 11{,}400$, 29 traits). Chrom-LOO protocol (test chroms $\{17, 18, 19, 20, 21, 22, X\}$).

\paragraph{Features.} CADD \citep{kircher2014cadd}, Borzoi\_L2\_L2 \citep{linder2025borzoi}, GPN-MSA\_absLLR \citep{benegas2023gpnmsa}. Four feature sets: CADD, Borzoi, CADD+Borzoi, CADD+GPN-MSA+Borzoi.

\paragraph{Metrics.} AUPRC weighted by test-chrom size (TraitGym leaderboard primary); marginal coverage; class-conditional coverage; \emph{$\shat$-bin coverage gap}, the max-over-bins absolute deviation of per-bin coverage from target $1 - \alpha$. All results are at $\alpha = 0.10$, seed 42, $K = 5$ unless noted.

\paragraph{Uncertainty quantification of the metrics themselves.} \texttt{HistGradientBoostingClassifier} at \texttt{max\_depth=2} with \texttt{early\_stopping=False} is \emph{deterministic} given fixed data: under our configuration (default \texttt{subsample=1.0}, \texttt{max\_features=None}, \texttt{max\_bins=255}) \texttt{random\_state} has no active injection point, and the headline AUPRC and coverage are identical across seeds $\{42, 7, 2024\}$. We ruled out the alternative hypothesis of data leakage via three orthogonal sanity checks (App.~\ref{app:data:leakage}): (i) training with 50\% random label corruption collapses AUPRC from $0.90$/$0.35$ to $0.147$/$0.126$, near the positive-class base rate; (ii) forcibly introducing row-level stochasticity via 80\% subsampling produces strictly positive seed variance (std $0.0006$ on Mendelian, $0.0027$ on Complex); (iii) the chrom-LOO split has zero train$\cap$test row overlap. We therefore report $95\%$ confidence intervals from chromosome-level bootstrap ($B = 200$ resamples with replacement of calibration chromosomes) in addition to the point estimates. The bootstrap captures \emph{calibration-set variability}, which is the deployment-relevant source of uncertainty.

\paragraph{Key reading.} (i) Our GBM aggregator alone gives SOTA Mendelian AUPRC $0.900$, a $+14.8$\,pp improvement over published LogReg \citep{benegas2025traitgym}. (ii) Adding AlphaGenome \citep{avsec2026alphagenome} as a 140-dimensional feature panel (RNA\_SEQ and DNASE across 8 tissues, App.~\ref{app:data:features}) lifts Mendelian AUPRC to $0.912$ ($+1.15$\,pp) with the $\shat$-bin gap dropping from $0.020$ to $0.018$; on Complex the AUPRC is essentially flat ($0.353 \to 0.352$) and the bootstrap CI on $\shat$-bin gap is identical to the non-AlphaGenome variant ($0.004 \pm 0.002$). The Mendelian-specific improvement is consistent with AlphaGenome's tissue-specific predictions being most informative for monogenic variants with dominant expression/splice effects, while polygenic GWAS complex-trait variants rely on dispersed long-range signals that our 8-tissue panel does not isolate. (iii) Class-conditional calibration (Day 10) fixes the minority-class pathology of split CP (Cov\,$|Y{=}1$ $0.54 \to 0.90$ on Complex) but leaves the \emph{local} gap untouched. (iv) HCCP's Mondrian-$(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ partition reduces the $\shat$-bin gap by a further $5\text{--}25\times$ to within one standard error of the finite-sample $1/(n_{k,b}+1)$ floor. (v) DEGU-lite \citep{zhou2026degu} learns $\shat$ but applies split CP rather than Mondrian, so its $\shat$-bin gap remains near the class-conditional level --- the conformal partition, not the $\shat$ head, is the load-bearing component.

\paragraph{Three observations.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{HCCP holds across all three axes}: $\shat$-bin gap $\leq 0.04$ in all settings except the strong-shift M$\to$C direction (see below). Chrom-LOO Mendelian CADD+Borzoi (gap $0.198$) is the residual pain point; CADD+GPN-MSA+Borzoi corrects it to $0.077$.

    \item \textbf{Cross-dataset shift reveals direction asymmetry.} Complex $\to$ Mendelian retains $\mathrm{AUPRC} = 0.665$ and $\shat$-bin gap $0.035$ --- evidence that the Complex feature space \emph{contains} the Mendelian one. T4's Barber 2023 Thm 2 proxy bound is tight on Mendelian $\to$ Complex CADD+Borzoi: observed coverage $0.738$ vs proxy $(1 - \alpha) - \hat d_{\text{TV}} = 0.742$ (1.4 std below).

\subsection{Ablations}

\paragraph{$K$ sweep (T3 finite-sample rate validation).} Across $K \in \{3, 5, 10, 20\}$ on Complex CADD+Borzoi: empirical $\shat$-bin gap grows as $K$ grows (smaller $n_{k,b}$), but stays below the theoretical $1/(n_{k,b}+1)$ bound (App.~\ref{app:tables}). $K = 5\text{--}10$ is the sweet spot.

\paragraph{$\shat$ source ablation.} We compare four heads: (a) supervised GBM on OOF residuals (HCCP default), (b) DEGU-lite bootstrap-GBM ensemble ($M = 10$, $\shat$ = ensemble std), (c) DEGU-actual CNN ensemble ($M = 5$, one-hot 200\,bp DNA windows centered on each variant, fixed test split matching TraitGym), (d) no $\shat$ (homoscedastic, Day 10). On Complex, HCCP (a) achieves gap $0.020$; DEGU-lite (b) gives $0.103$ --- \emph{$5\times$ worse}. The failure mode of (b) is interpretable: bin 0 (low ensemble std, i.e.\ high agreement) has coverage $0.570$. All 10 bootstrap students agree \emph{but are wrong together}; ensemble disagreement captures epistemic uncertainty but not the aleatoric residual structure our score requires. DEGU-actual (c) produces a valid $\shat$ signal (ensemble std $0.023 \pm 0.044$ on Mendelian), but its base predictor fails the point-estimate step: the $M = 5$ CNN ensemble on 200\,bp one-hot windows reaches only AUPRC $0.140$ (vs HCCP GBM $0.900$, a $6.4\times$ gap). Raw-sequence CNN on variant pathogenicity is not competitive at TraitGym scale --- a finding consistent with the fact that DEGU's original paper \citep{zhou2026degu} reports no TraitGym number and evaluates exclusively on MPRA regression, where sequence-to-activity is a direct mapping. This reverse-validates HCCP's design decision to aggregate pre-computed genomic features (CADD/Borzoi/GPN-MSA) rather than learn from raw DNA end-to-end.

\paragraph{$\shat$ target-function ablation.} Within the supervised-$\shat$ family, we compare two training targets: (i) absolute residual $r_i = |y_i - \phat^{(-C_i)}(x_i)|$ with squared-error loss (HCCP default, Gaussian NLL for the half-normal approximation), and (ii) log-variance $\log(r_i^2 + \epsilon)$ with squared error (DEGU-style reparameterization). On Mendelian CADD+GPN-MSA+Borzoi at $\alpha = 0.10$ and $K = 5$, target (i) achieves $\shat$-bin gap $0.077$ and singleton fraction $0.80$; target (ii) achieves gap $0.109$ and singleton fraction $0.83$. The absolute-residual target achieves tighter local coverage at a $3$ pp cost in decisiveness. We attribute the gap to the fact that the log-variance reparameterization amplifies small residuals near zero (where most benign variants sit), producing an overconfident low-$\shat$ tail; the absolute-residual target preserves the nonconformity-score scale directly.

\paragraph{Nonconformity score ablation.} We compare four score functions under identical Mondrian-$(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ calibration with fixed $\shat$: (A) HCCP default $|y - \phat|/\shat$, (B) squared $(y - \phat)^2/\shat^2$, (C) Gaussian-CDF tail $\Phi(|y - \phat|/\shat)$, (D) $\shat$-free $|y - \phat|$. Results on Mendelian CADD+GPN-MSA+Borzoi (single seed, $K = 5$) and Complex: the $\shat$-bin gap is nearly indistinguishable between (A), (B), (D) on both datasets (0.009/0.010/0.007 Mendelian; 0.001/0.001/0.003 Complex) because the Mondrian partition already conditions on $\shat$; however, the per-chromosome max coverage gap favors the $\shat$-normalized scores (A)/(B) (0.100) over (D) (0.133) on Mendelian, confirming that $\shat$ in the score provides cross-chrom robustness beyond what the $\shat$-bin partition captures. Score (C) fails on Mendelian ($\shat$-gap $0.104$, singleton $0.60$) because $\Phi$ saturates at $1$ for large inputs, compressing the anomalous tail and widening the prediction set unnecessarily. We recommend (A) as the canonical HCCP score; (B) is a valid monotone alternative (Lemma~\ref{lem:rescaling}); (D) is acceptable when the Mondrian partition is already sufficient.

\paragraph{Class-conditional on/off.} With class-cond on and no $\shat$-bin stratification, gap on Complex is $0.51$ (Day 10). With $\shat$-bin on but no class-cond, minority-class coverage collapses (Cov\,$|Y{=}1 = 0.54$). Both are needed; the product partition is not decomposable.

\paragraph{Benchmark audit byproduct.} CADD-alone LogReg on Mendelian reaches AUPRC $0.871$, exceeding TraitGym's published ``SOTA'' CADD+Borzoi LogReg of $0.752$ \citep{benegas2025traitgym}. We report this as an internal inconsistency in the TraitGym pipeline, with our GBM aggregator closing the gap to $0.900$.

\subsection{Honest limitations}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Marginal-score TV proxy.} The Barber 2023 Thm 2 bound uses residual-swap TV; we estimate it via score-marginal TV, an approximation exact only under i.i.d.\ but underestimating for cross-dataset shift. On CADD+GPN-MSA+Borzoi M$\to$C the proxy is violated by $0.036$. Future work: direct residual-swap TV estimation (§\ref{sec:discussion}).
    \item \textbf{DEGU reimpl caveat.} We compare against two DEGU variants: DEGU-lite (GBM-backbone, $M = 10$ bootstrap students, shared backbone with HCCP for like-for-like $\shat$-source ablation) and DEGU-actual (CNN-backbone, $M = 5$ one-hot 200\,bp ensemble, matching the original paper's architecture). DEGU-actual's base AUPRC on TraitGym is $0.140$/$c_{\mathrm{Complex}}$ --- not competitive with GBM-on-features SOTA at $0.900$ Mendelian --- so the fair $\shat$-only comparison is against DEGU-lite at matched point-estimate quality; DEGU-actual is reported in App.~\ref{app:tables} as an honest negative result on end-to-end CNN VEP.
    \item \textbf{A3-loc (local stationarity) cannot be fully verified} at our sample sizes. Our cell-level KS audit (12.3\% rejection at $p < 0.05$, App.~\ref{app:data}) is the closest empirical analog. T3-loc is stated conditionally on A3-loc throughout.
\end{itemize}
