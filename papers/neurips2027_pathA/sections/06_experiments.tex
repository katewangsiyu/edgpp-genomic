\subsection{Head-to-head against contemporary CP}
\label{sec:experiments:h2h}

We compare HCCP (B4) against six contemporary CP variants at matched $(\phat, \shat)$: split (B1), $\shat$-Mondrian (B2, \citep{bostrom2020mondrian}), class-Mondrian (B3, \citep{sadinle2019lac}), RLCP \citep{hore2025rlcp}, weighted CP \citep{tibshirani2019weighted}, and SC-CP \citep{vanderlaan2024sccp}. \textbf{At the recommended operating point ($K_{\mathrm{eval}} = 3$ Mendelian, $5$ Complex; per-cell minority $\geq 100$), HCCP is the only single-fold partition we evaluated that holds $\mathrm{cov}_{|Y=1} \geq 0.85$ and $\shat$-bin gap $\leq 0.20$ on both datasets} (Tab.~\ref{tab:h2h}, Tab.~\ref{tab:cp_baselines}; all rows $B=200$ chrom-bootstrap mean $\pm$ std). HCCP tightens the bin-local gap by $13.7\times$ on Complex / $2.3\times$ on Mendelian over the on-axis baseline B2 $\shat$-Mondrian (HCCP $0.060$/$0.173$ vs.\ B2 $0.827$/$0.401$, bootstrap means), and $13.4\times$/$2.4\times$ over the off-axis B3 class-Mondrian. The two ratios coincide because B2 $\shat$-stratification still loses minority coverage ($\mathrm{cov}_{|Y=1} = 0.624$ on Complex), so its $\shat$-bin gap is dominated by minority-cell undercoverage --- the joint $(y \times \shat)$ product, not the $\shat$ axis alone, is what binds. The Mendelian advantage narrows under weighted CP (HCCP $0.173 \pm 0.192$ vs.\ wCP $0.252 \pm 0.153$, $1.5\times$); a $K_{\mathrm{eval}} \in \{2,3,5,8,10\}$ sweep (App.~\ref{app:tables:keval_sensitivity}) shows HCCP dominates the full range on Complex ($3$--$34\times$) but reverses on Mendelian at $K_{\mathrm{eval}} \geq 5$ (per-cell minority $< 70$). The same calibration on the published LogReg aggregator yields essentially identical $\shat$-bin gap (App.~\ref{app:tables:hccp_logreg}), decoupling the conformal-side gain from base-predictor accuracy.

\paragraph{Imbalance scaling validates T5's $\pi_{\min}$ dependence.}
Subsampling Complex to $\pi_{+} \in \{0.05, 0.10, 0.20, 0.30, 0.50\}$, B1 and B2 minority-coverage degrades monotonically as $\pi_{+} \to 0$ ($0.587 \to 0.810$ for B1; $0.650 \to 0.878$ for B2), while HCCP and class-Mondrian hold at the target across the full sweep (App.~\ref{app:tables}, Tab.~\ref{tab:imbsweep}) — the empirical realisation of the $\pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ factor in T5's bound.

\subsection{Three-axis OOD + cross-domain stress test}
\label{sec:experiments:ood}

We evaluate HCCP on three orthogonal distributional axes (chrom-LOO, trait-LOO, cross-dataset) and a cross-domain benchmark (ProteinGym). \emph{Trait-LOO} reaches the finite-sample $1/(n_{k,b}+1)$ floor on both datasets ($\shat$-bin gap $\leq 0.004$), with one honest exception: the Skeletal/connective OMIM cluster ($n_{\mathrm{tot}} = 470$) shows $\mathrm{cov}_{|Y=1} = 0.745$ ($1/3$ Mendelian clusters fall below the $0.85$ minority-coverage target). \emph{Chrom-LOO} on the SOTA feature set: gap $\leq 0.077$ Mendelian and $0.023$ Complex. \emph{Cross-dataset} C$\to$M holds (gap $0.035$); the reverse M$\to$C direction is reported as a failure-mode case study (§\ref{sec:experiments:failure_mode}). \emph{ProteinGym cross-domain} \citep{notin2023proteingym}: across 50 assay-LOO held-out proteins, HCCP attains marginal coverage $0.887$, within $\pm 0.02$ of target on $27/50$ assays and within $\pm 0.05$ on $44/50$. The $6$ outlier assays correlate with low base-predictor AUPRC (Spearman $\rho = 0.46$, $p = 0.001$), confirming coverage failures track base-predictor weakness rather than conformal failure (full diagnosis in App.~\ref{app:proteingym}).

\subsection{Failure mode: M$\to$C cross-dataset}
\label{sec:experiments:failure_mode}

The reverse cross-dataset direction (Mendelian-trained $\to$ Complex-tested) jointly violates HCCP's two preconditions: (i) the base predictor collapses (AUPRC $0.205$ vs.\ in-distribution $0.353$), and (ii) calibration-fold exchangeability fails at the dataset level (ClinVar pathogenicity vs.\ GWAS catalog selection criteria; \citet{tibshirani2019weighted}). Marginal coverage drops to $0.738$, within $1.4$ std of the Barber 2023 Thm 2 marginal-coverage proxy bound ($0.742$). We do not claim T3$'$ binds the bin-conditional gap in this regime; this is the joint-failure boundary that delineates where HCCP's coverage machinery is operational. The forward C$\to$M direction (in §\ref{sec:experiments:ood}) and ProteinGym cross-domain do not violate either precondition, and HCCP holds.

\subsection{Ablations}

Ablations on $K$-sweep (T5 rate validation), $\shat$ source (supervised vs.\ DEGU-lite ensemble), $\shat$ target function (residual vs.\ log-variance), nonconformity score, class-conditional on/off, AlphaGenome \citep{avsec2026alphagenome} feature extension, and synthetic $n$-sweep (verifying the $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate of Theorem~\ref{thm:t5oracle}) are reported in App.~\ref{app:tables} and App.~\ref{app:synthetic}. The synthetic sweep is the direct test of T5.1's dimension-free claim: log-log slope $-0.50 \pm 0.05$ across $d \in \{8, 32, 128\}$ matches the $\pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ predicted constant. The DEGU-lite ablation \citep{zhou2026degu} swaps our supervised residual $\shat$ for an ensemble-distilled Gaussian-uncertainty $\shat$ at matched GBM backbone and matched HCCP-style Mondrian calibration; this isolates the choice of variance source within HCCP and is \emph{not} a head-to-head against DEGU's published Keras CNN MPRA pipeline. With this caveat, the supervised residual head outperforms the distilled ensemble head by $4.3\times$ on Complex worst-cell coverage, identifying where aleatoric-aware $\shat$ binds (polygenic complex traits) and where ensemble disagreement suffices (monogenic Mendelian).
