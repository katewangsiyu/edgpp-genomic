\subsection{Setup}

\paragraph{Datasets.} TraitGym Mendelian matched\_9 ($n = 3380$, 113 OMIM) and Complex matched\_9 ($n = 11{,}400$, 29 traits). Chrom-LOO protocol (test chroms $\{17, 18, 19, 20, 21, 22, X\}$).

\paragraph{Features.} CADD \citep{kircher2014cadd}, Borzoi\_L2\_L2 \citep{linder2025borzoi}, GPN-MSA\_absLLR \citep{benegas2023gpnmsa}. Four feature sets: CADD, Borzoi, CADD+Borzoi, CADD+GPN-MSA+Borzoi.

\paragraph{Metrics.} AUPRC weighted by test-chrom size (TraitGym leaderboard primary); marginal coverage; class-conditional coverage; \emph{$\shat$-bin coverage gap}, the max-over-bins absolute deviation of per-bin coverage from target $1 - \alpha$. All results are at $\alpha = 0.10$, seed 42, $K = 5$ unless noted.

\paragraph{Uncertainty quantification of the metrics themselves.}
\texttt{HistGradientBoostingClassifier} at \texttt{max\_depth=2} with \texttt{early\_stopping=False} is \emph{deterministic} given fixed data. Under our configuration (default \texttt{subsample=1.0}, \texttt{max\_features=None}, \texttt{max\_bins=255}), \texttt{random\_state} has no active injection point, and the headline AUPRC and coverage are identical across seeds $\{42, 7, 2024\}$. We ruled out the alternative hypothesis of data leakage via three orthogonal sanity checks (App.~\ref{app:data:leakage}): (i) training with 50\% random label corruption collapses AUPRC from $0.90$ / $0.35$ to $0.147$ / $0.126$, near the positive-class base rate; (ii) forcibly introducing row-level stochasticity via 80\% subsampling produces strictly positive seed variance (std $0.0006$ on Mendelian, $0.0027$ on Complex); (iii) the chrom-LOO split has zero train-test row overlap. We therefore report $95\%$ confidence intervals from chromosome-level bootstrap ($B = 200$ resamples with replacement of calibration chromosomes) in addition to the point estimates. The bootstrap captures \emph{calibration-set variability}, which is the deployment-relevant source of uncertainty.

\subsection{Joint frontier: head-to-head with off-the-shelf normalized + Mondrian CP}
\label{sec:experiments:h2h}

The three preceding rows of Tab.~\ref{tab:main} (split-CP, class-conditional, DEGU-lite + split) characterise HCCP's improvement over historical TraitGym pipelines. To address whether HCCP also improves on \emph{contemporary} normalized-Mondrian CP, we run all four reasonable single-axis variants on top of the same $(\phat, \shat)$ pair, using the \texttt{crepes} \citep{bostrom2022crepes} reference implementation:
\begin{description}
    \item[B1] vanilla normalized split CP (no Mondrian);
    \item[B2] $\shat$-Mondrian \citep{bostrom2020mondrian}: bins along $\shat$ only;
    \item[B3] class-Mondrian \citep{sadinle2019lac}: bins along $y$ only;

\end{description}

\paragraph{Imbalance sweep validates T5's $\pi_{\min}$ dependence.}
Tab.~\ref{tab:imbsweep} subsamples Complex to $\pi_{+} \in \{0.05, 0.10, 0.20, 0.30, 0.50\}$ (positives kept whole; negatives subsampled or vice versa) and re-runs the four variants under proper nested chrom-LOO $K$-selection. B1 (split CP) and B2 ($\shat$-Mondrian) minority-coverage \emph{degrades monotonically} as $\pi_{+} \to 0$ ($0.587 \to 0.810$ for B1; $0.650 \to 0.878$ for B2), while B3 and B4 hold at the target across the full sweep --- the empirical realisation of the $\pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ factor in T5's bound $G(K^\star) \leq 2\sqrt{L_F R / (\pi_{\min} n)}$. Fig.~\ref{fig:frontier} visualises the same data as a joint frontier scatter. Mendelian replicates the same qualitative pattern at $\pi_{+} \in \{0.10, 0.20, 0.30\}$ (the sample-size-stable regime: $n_{+} \geq 86$ per cell): B2's $\mathrm{cov}_{|Y=1}$ ranges $0.840$–$0.873$ while HCCP holds at $0.876$–$0.893$; B1 over-covers the minority on Mendelian (cov$_{|Y=1} \approx 0.95$) but at the cost of $\shat$-bin gap $\geq 0.37$ throughout. At the extreme imbalance points ($\pi_{+} = 0.05$, $n_{+} \approx 17$ per cell) and the dropped-negatives extreme ($\pi_{+} = 0.50$, $n = 676$), all four methods are dominated by the per-cell sample-size floor $n_{k,b} \geq \lceil 1/\alpha\rceil$ and the metric saturates; we read these endpoints as binomial-noise-limited rather than as method comparisons.

\subsection{Ablations}

\paragraph{$K$ sweep (T3 finite-sample rate validation; $\hat K_{\mathrm{CV}}$ selection).} Across $K \in \{2, 3, 5, 8, 10, 15, 20\}$ on Complex CADD+Borzoi: empirical $\shat$-bin gap grows as $K$ grows (variance term $K/(\pi_{\min} n)$ of T5 dominates), staying below the theoretical $1/(n_{k,b}+1)$ bound (App.~\ref{app:tables:ksweep}). The CV-selected $\hat K_{\mathrm{CV}} = 2$ (Complex) and $\hat K_{\mathrm{CV}} = 3$ (Mendelian) outperform the $K = 5$ default by $6\text{--}7\times$ on worst-cell gap (consistent with §\ref{sec:method} and §\ref{sec:theory:t5}). The headline tables (Tables~\ref{tab:main}, \ref{tab:threeaxis}) are reported at $K = 5$ for direct comparability with the prior split-CP and class-conditional baselines (which use the same default); the $\hat K_{\mathrm{CV}}$ row is in App.~\ref{app:tables:ksweep}. Figure~\ref{fig:k_sweep} (§\ref{sec:theory:t5}) overlays the empirical mean- and worst-cell gaps against the T5 bias-variance bound.

\paragraph{$n$-sweep on synthetic data (T5 rate validation).} Theorem~\ref{thm:t5oracle} predicts $G(K^\star) = O(n^{-1/2})$ with constant $\propto \pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ and \emph{independent of feature dimension $d$}. Because TraitGym is fixed at $n = 3{,}380$ (Mendelian) and $n = 11{,}400$ (Complex), the rate cannot be verified directly. We sample synthetic data from a Gaussian-shift family with oracle variance $\sigma^\star(x) = 0.1 + 0.4|x_1|$ (1-D heteroscedasticity along $x_1$), Lipschitz constant $L_F = 4$, sweeping $n \in \{500, 1{,}000, 2{,}000, 4{,}000, 8{,}000, 16{,}000, 32{,}000\}$, $d \in \{8, 32, 128\}$, $\pi_{\min} \in \{0.1, 0.5\}$, with 5 replicates per cell (App.~\ref{app:synthetic}). Figure~\ref{fig:n_sweep} reports the empirical worst-cell gap $G(\hat K_{\mathrm{CV}})$. The empirical curves track the theoretical $O(n^{-1/2})$ slope across all three feature dimensions $d$ — the dimension-free claim of T5.1 is visible as the three dimension curves overlapping inside each $\pi$ panel — and the $\pi_{\min} = 0.10$ panel sits above the $\pi_{\min} = 0.50$ panel by a factor of $\approx 2$ at matched $n$, close to the predicted $\sqrt{5} \approx 2.24$ from T5.1's $\pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ constant. At $d = 32$, growing $n$ from $500$ to $32{,}000$ shrinks $G(\hat K_{\mathrm{CV}})$ from $0.092$ to $0.023$ ($\pi_{\min} = 0.10$) and from $0.075$ to $0.010$ ($\pi_{\min} = 0.50$).

\paragraph{$\shat$ source ablation.} We compare three heads: (a) supervised GBM on OOF residuals (HCCP default), (b) DEGU-lite bootstrap-GBM ensemble ($M = 10$, $\shat$ = ensemble std), (c) no $\shat$ (homoscedastic, Day 10). \textbf{Scope.} \citet{zhou2026degu}'s original DEGU is evaluated on MPRA regression and is not claimed for variant classification on TraitGym; we therefore compare against DEGU's $\shat$ \emph{mechanism} (ensemble distillation) at matched GBM backbone for a like-for-like ablation, and do not reimplement DEGU's CNN end-to-end on a task it does not target. On Complex CADD+Borzoi at $K = 5$, HCCP (a) achieves gap $0.020$; DEGU-lite (b) gives $0.103$; the matched-feature-set comparison on CADD+GPN-MSA+Borzoi (App.~\ref{app:tables:degu}, Table~\ref{tab:degu}) gives the canonical ratio $4.3\times$ ($0.057$ vs $0.013$). The two $\shat$ sources are informative in complementary regimes: bin 0 of DEGU-lite (low ensemble std, i.e.\ high agreement) carries coverage $0.570$, indicating that all 10 bootstrap students agree but the underlying variants still carry residual aleatoric uncertainty that ensemble disagreement does not see. Ensemble $\shat$ remains the right primitive when epistemic uncertainty dominates (small training set, model misspecification); supervised residual $\shat$ wins on the polygenic regime where aleatoric noise is the binding constraint.

\paragraph{$\shat$ target-function ablation.} Within the supervised-$\shat$ family, we compare two training targets: (i) absolute residual $r_i = |y_i - \phat^{(-C_i)}(x_i)|$ with squared-error loss (HCCP default, Gaussian NLL for the half-normal approximation), and (ii) log-variance $\log(r_i^2 + \epsilon)$ with squared error (DEGU-style reparameterization). On Mendelian CADD+GPN-MSA+Borzoi at $\alpha = 0.10$ and $K = 5$, target (i) achieves $\shat$-bin gap $0.077$ and singleton fraction $0.80$; target (ii) achieves gap $0.109$ and singleton fraction $0.83$. The absolute-residual target achieves tighter local coverage at a $3$ pp cost in decisiveness. We attribute the gap to the fact that the log-variance reparameterization amplifies small residuals near zero (where most benign variants sit), producing an overconfident low-$\shat$ tail; the absolute-residual target preserves the nonconformity-score scale directly.

\paragraph{Nonconformity score ablation.}
We compare four score functions under identical Mondrian-$(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ calibration with fixed $\shat$:
(A) HCCP default $|y - \phat|/\shat$;
(B) squared $(y - \phat)^2/\shat^2$;
(C) Gaussian-CDF tail $\Phi(|y - \phat|/\shat)$;
(D) $\shat$-free $|y - \phat|$.
On Mendelian and Complex with the CADD+GPN-MSA+Borzoi feature set ($K = 5$, single seed), the $\shat$-bin gap is nearly indistinguishable across (A), (B), and (D): $0.009 / 0.010 / 0.007$ on Mendelian and $0.001 / 0.001 / 0.003$ on Complex, because the Mondrian partition already conditions on $\shat$. The per-chromosome maximum coverage gap, however, favours the $\shat$-normalised scores (A) and (B) ($0.100$) over (D) ($0.133$) on Mendelian, confirming that $\shat$ in the score provides cross-chromosome robustness beyond what the $\shat$-bin partition alone captures. Score (C) fails on Mendelian ($\shat$-gap $0.104$, singleton $0.60$) because $\Phi$ saturates at $1$ for large inputs, compressing the anomalous tail and widening the prediction set unnecessarily. We recommend (A) as the canonical HCCP score; (B) is a valid monotone alternative (Lemma~\ref{lem:rescaling}); (D) is acceptable when the Mondrian partition is already sufficient.

\paragraph{Class-conditional on/off.} With class-cond on and no $\shat$-bin stratification, gap on Complex is $0.51$ (Day 10). With $\shat$-bin on but no class-cond, minority-class coverage collapses (Cov\,$|Y{=}1 = 0.54$). Both are needed; the product partition is not decomposable.

\paragraph{Feature extension ablation: AlphaGenome integration.} As a stress test of HCCP's feature-additivity across heterogeneous foundation models, we evaluate the framework with the AlphaGenome \citep{avsec2026alphagenome} 140-dimensional RNA-SEQ + DNASE panel (8 tissues; App.~\ref{app:data:features}) appended to the SOTA feature set. On Mendelian, the addition lifts AUPRC by $+1.15$\,pp ($0.900 \to 0.912$) and tightens the $\shat$-bin gap from $0.020$ to $0.018$. On Complex, AUPRC is flat ($0.353 \to 0.352$) and the gap is unchanged within bootstrap CI ($0.004 \pm 0.002$). The asymmetry is consistent with AlphaGenome's tissue-specific predictions favouring monogenic variants with dominant splice/expression effects; polygenic complex-trait variants rely on dispersed long-range signals that an 8-tissue panel does not isolate. We report this as evidence that HCCP's coverage machinery is invariant to the choice of underlying genomic feature pipeline, not as a deployment recommendation; the headline numbers in Table~\ref{tab:main} use the SOTA feature set without AlphaGenome.

\paragraph{Benchmark audit byproduct.} CADD-alone LogReg on Mendelian reaches AUPRC $0.871$, exceeding TraitGym's published ``SOTA'' CADD+Borzoi LogReg of $0.752$ \citep{benegas2025traitgym}. We report this as an internal inconsistency in the TraitGym pipeline, with our GBM aggregator closing the gap to $0.900$.

\subsection{Failure mode characterization: M$\to$C cross-dataset}
\label{sec:experiments:failure_mode}

The reverse cross-dataset direction (Mendelian-trained $\to$ Complex-tested) is a regime where HCCP's coverage machinery and base predictor jointly fail. We report it as a diagnostic case study, not as a deployment endorsement.

\paragraph{Numbers.} On CADD+Borzoi: AUPRC $0.205$, marginal coverage $0.738$, $\shat$-bin gap $0.331$. On CADD+GPN-MSA+Borzoi: AUPRC $0.203$, marginal coverage $0.727$, $\shat$-bin gap $0.530$. The $\shat$-bin gap is one to two orders of magnitude larger than the in-distribution Complex gap of $0.020$–$0.023$.

\paragraph{Diagnosis.} Two failures compound. (i) The base predictor itself collapses (AUPRC $0.205$ vs in-distribution $0.353$), because the Mendelian-trained GBM aggregator has not seen the polygenic feature distribution that dominates Complex variants. Conformal calibration cannot rescue a base predictor that has lost discrimination. (ii) Assumption A1$'$ (chrom-group exchangeability between calibration and test folds) is violated at the dataset level: Mendelian and Complex sample variants under different selection criteria (ClinVar pathogenicity vs.\ GWAS catalog), so the calibration fold's score distribution is no longer exchangeable with the test fold's. T4's Barber 2023 Thm 2 marginal-coverage proxy correctly predicts the coverage drop: observed marginal $0.738$ vs.\ proxy $(1 - \alpha) - \hat d_{\mathrm{TV}} = 0.742$ on CADD+Borzoi (1.4 std below). The marginal proxy does not extend to bin-conditional coverage, and we make no claim that T3$'$ binds the $0.530$ $\shat$-bin gap on CADD+GPN-MSA+Borzoi.

\paragraph{What this characterizes.} HCCP's guarantees are conditional on (i) a base predictor that retains discriminative signal and (ii) calibration-fold exchangeability with the test point. M$\to$C violates both. We flag this as the joint-failure regime to delineate the boundary of what HCCP claims, not to suggest the boundary can be moved without strengthening the assumptions or the base predictor. Cross-domain transfer in the C$\to$M direction (§\ref{sec:experiments}) and to ProteinGym (App.~\ref{app:proteingym}) holds; the M$\to$C direction does not, and we do not endorse it as a deployment scenario.

\subsection{Honest limitations}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Marginal-score TV proxy.} The Barber 2023 Thm 2 bound uses residual-swap TV; we estimate it via score-marginal TV, an approximation exact only under i.i.d.\ but underestimating for cross-dataset shift. On CADD+GPN-MSA+Borzoi M$\to$C the proxy is violated by $0.036$. Future work: direct residual-swap TV estimation (§\ref{sec:discussion}).
    \item \textbf{DEGU comparison scope.} \citet{zhou2026degu}'s DEGU is evaluated on MPRA regression and does not claim a TraitGym number; we compare against DEGU's $\shat$-mechanism (ensemble distillation) at matched GBM backbone (DEGU-lite, $M = 10$) for like-for-like ablation. We do not port DEGU's CNN end-to-end to TraitGym, since reproducing it on a task the original paper does not target would not be informative about DEGU's capacity.
    \item \textbf{A3-loc (local stationarity) cannot be fully verified} at our sample sizes. Our cell-level KS audit (12.3\% rejection at $p < 0.05$, App.~\ref{app:data}) is the closest empirical analog. T3-loc is stated conditionally on A3-loc throughout.
\end{itemize}
