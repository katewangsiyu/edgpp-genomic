\paragraph{Clinical variant interpretation needs more than a score.}
Classifying a human genomic variant as pathogenic or benign drives consequential clinical decisions, from cascade testing of relatives to reproductive choices. The ACMG/AMP consensus guidelines \citep{richards2015acmg} structure this decision as a weighted aggregation of evidence categories rather than as a point estimate. Modern machine-learned variant effect predictors (VEPs) collapse that structure into a single probability \citep{kircher2014cadd,linder2025borzoi,benegas2023gpnmsa,benegas2025traitgym}, which clinicians then threshold downstream. This presents the user with a number that lacks an explicit notion of confidence: the same $\phat(x) = 0.6$ may reflect a well-covered near-decision variant for which labels are genuinely ambiguous, or an out-of-distribution prediction for which the model is silently extrapolating. What is missing is a prediction \emph{set} with finite-sample coverage guarantees that remain informative when the data-generating regime shifts across chromosomes, traits, or disease architectures.

\paragraph{Existing uncertainty quantification for VEP is either absent or marginal-only.}
The TraitGym benchmark \citep{benegas2025traitgym}, the current standard for evaluating VEP aggregators on 3{,}380 Mendelian and 11{,}400 complex-trait variants, reports only point AUPRC and provides no calibration of its LogReg aggregator. DEGU \citep{zhou2026degu} introduces a heteroscedastic variance head for MPRA regression via deep ensemble distillation and gestures at conformal prediction in the abstract, but neither formalises the guarantee nor transfers it to variant classification. Conformal prediction itself is mature: split CP \citep{vovk2005algorithmic}, Mondrian CP \citep{vovk2003mondrian}, CQR for regression \citep{romano2019cqr}, jackknife+ \citep{barber2021jackknife}, and CP beyond exchangeability \citep{barber2023exchangeability} cover a wide design space, and Barber et~al.~\citep{barber2020limits} rule out pointwise conditional coverage in finite samples. What is \emph{not} in prior art is a composition that (i) consumes a learned $\shat(x)$ the way CQR consumes a quantile regressor, (ii) stratifies calibration by both label and $\shat$-bin to regain as much conditionality as finite samples allow, and (iii) carries local coverage guarantees across the distribution shifts that define real VEP deployment. The gap is especially load-bearing for VEP: the minority-class (pathogenic) positive rate is $\approx 10\%$, $\phat(x)$ is sharply heteroscedastic across the $\shat$-bins we identify, and chrom-LOO + trait-LOO + cross-dataset shift are intrinsic to any clinical rollout.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
Figure~\ref{fig:concept} summarizes the pipeline. Our contributions, in order of theoretical depth:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Optimal Mondrian granularity for heteroscedastic classification} (§\ref{sec:theory:t5}, App.~\ref{app:proofs}): Theorem~\ref{thm:t5oracle} (T5) proves that for Mondrian conformal classification stratified by a learned $L_F$-Lipschitz variance head $\shat$, the worst-cell coverage gap satisfies $G(K) \leq L_F R / K + K / (\pi_{\min} n)$ and is minimized at $K^\star = \lfloor\sqrt{L_F R \pi_{\min} n}\rfloor$, yielding $G(K^\star) = O(n^{-1/2})$ \emph{with constant independent of the feature dimension}. Theorem~\ref{thm:t5lower} (T8) constructs a Gaussian-shift family establishing the matching $\Omega(n^{-1/2})$ lower bound, so the rate is tight. To our knowledge this is the first bias--variance analysis of the Mondrian bin count under a learned variance head with a matching minimax rate.
    \item \textbf{HCCP, an instantiation with finite-sample layered guarantees} (§\ref{sec:method}, \ref{sec:theory:t1t2}--\ref{sec:theory:t3}): a gradient-boosted aggregator $\phat$ over pre-trained genomic features, a reliability head $\shat$ trained under Gaussian NLL on chrom-LOO out-of-fold residuals, and Mondrian conformal calibration stratified by $(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$. The nonconformity score $s(x,y) = |y - \phat(x)| / \shat(x)$ is the binary-classification analog of CQR \citep{romano2019cqr}. HCCP is the \emph{smallest modification of split CP} that simultaneously admits (T1) marginal, (T2) class-conditional, and (T3) bin-conditional coverage with finite-sample rate $1/(n_{k,b}+1)$, while remaining compatible with \citet{barber2020limits}'s pointwise impossibility. Robust lower-bound counterparts T1$'$/T2$'$/T3$'$ follow from \citet{barber2023exchangeability} Theorem~2, and (T4) provides the chrom-shift robustness bound.
    \item \textbf{Empirical three-axis OOD validation plus cross-benchmark stress test} (§\ref{sec:experiments}, App.~\ref{app:proteingym}): on TraitGym Mendelian ($n = 3{,}380$) and Complex ($n = 11{,}400$), HCCP keeps the $\shat$-bin coverage gap $\leq 0.04$ across chromosome-LOO, trait-LOO (30 OMIMs, 28 complex traits), and cross-dataset shifts, \emph{reaching} the finite-sample floor $0.002$–$0.004$ under trait-LOO. The $\alpha$ sweep (App.~\ref{app:alpha}) confirms the guarantee is not an artifact of the $\alpha = 0.10$ headline. HCCP reduces the $\shat$-bin gap by $5$–$25\times$ over plain Mondrian class-conditional calibration and over our DEGU reimplementation (DEGU-lite) at matched AUPRC; the conformal partition, not the variance head in isolation, is the load-bearing component ($4.3\times$ gap improvement over ensemble $\shat$ on Complex). A cross-domain replication on \textbf{ProteinGym} \citep{notin2023proteingym} (50 held-out protein assays, de novo physicochemical features, no genomic foundation models) reproduces coverage $0.887 \pm 0.028$ (mean $\pm$ std across proteins) and $\shat$-bin gap median $0.042$, within an order of magnitude of TraitGym's chrom-LOO result --- establishing that the coverage machinery is a property of the conformal calibration, not of any specific feature pipeline.
    \item \textbf{Benchmark audit byproduct} (§\ref{sec:experiments}): the GBM aggregator on TraitGym features reaches AUPRC $0.900$ on Mendelian, a $+14.8$\,pp improvement over the published LogReg \citep{benegas2025traitgym}; a secondary internal inconsistency (CADD-alone LogReg at $0.871$, exceeding the published CADD+Borzoi SOTA of $0.752$) is documented for reproducibility.
\end{enumerate}

The result is a post-hoc framework with a matching-rate theoretical guarantee and a drop-in implementation that consumes any probabilistic VEP and returns prediction sets with three layers of coverage (marginal, class-conditional, and $\shat$-bin-local), all of which degrade gracefully under the distribution shifts that clinical deployment unavoidably induces.
