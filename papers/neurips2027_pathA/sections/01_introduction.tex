Clinical variant interpretation drives consequential decisions \citep{richards2015acmg}, yet machine-learned variant effect predictors (VEPs) collapse evidence into a single probability \citep{kircher2014cadd,linder2025borzoi,benegas2023gpnmsa,benegas2025traitgym} without explicit confidence. Conformal prediction guarantees distribution-free coverage \citep{vovk2005algorithmic,vovk2003mondrian,romano2019cqr,barber2021jackknife,barber2023exchangeability}, yet \citet{barber2020limits} rule out pointwise conditional coverage in finite samples. VEP therefore needs a method that (i) consumes a learned heteroscedastic head $\shat(x)$ as in DEGU \citep{zhou2026degu}, (ii) stratifies calibration by both label and $\shat$-bin to recover as much conditioning as finite samples permit, and (iii) holds local coverage across distribution shifts. The gap binds for VEP: TraitGym minority prevalence sits at $\pi_{\min} \approx 0.10$, and chrom-LOO, trait-LOO, and cross-dataset shifts are intrinsic.

The technical question is: which Mondrian partition simultaneously delivers (i) class-conditional coverage under extreme imbalance and (ii) bin-local coverage under heteroscedasticity? % [De-AI] Original: Single-axis answers fail individually --- $\shat$-Mondrian ...
Single-axis answers fail individually: $\shat$-Mondrian \citep{bostrom2020mondrian} attains local coverage in shape but loses minority-class coverage ($\mathrm{cov}_{|Y=1} = 0.62$ on TraitGym Complex; the resulting minority-cell undercoverage drives its $\shat$-bin gap up to $0.83$); class-Mondrian \citep{sadinle2019lac} attains class coverage but leaves a bin-local gap $13.4\times$ wider than the joint partition on Complex (bootstrap mean, $B=200$). Within the equi-bin Mondrian-$K$ family, we prove the joint $(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ Mondrian attains both with worst-cell coverage gap $G(K^\star) \leq 2\sqrt{L_F R / (\pi_{\min} n)}$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:t5oracle}) and a matching $\Omega(\sqrt{L_F R / (\pi_{\min} n)})$ finite-sample lower bound (Theorem~\ref{thm:t5lower}); $\pi_{\min}$ enters \emph{explicitly} as a $\pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ constant.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Tight finite-sample rate within the equi-bin Mondrian-$K$ class with $\pi_{\min}$ explicit in the constant} (Theorems~\ref{thm:t5oracle}, \ref{thm:t5lower}). To our knowledge this is the first finite-sample bound for class-conditional + heteroscedastic-local CP in which the minority-class probability $\pi_{\min}$ appears in the rate; the constant is dimension-free under a 1-D Lipschitz assumption on $\shat$. Concurrent imbalanced-CP work \citep{xie2025imbalancedcp,ding2023classcond,shi2024augmentedrank} provides marginal or class-cond rates without the $\pi_{\min}$-local product.
% [Polish] Original: HCCP extends DEGU's heteroscedastic head from MPRA regression to binary variant classification with finite-sample local coverage,
    \item \textbf{HCCP, a post-hoc framework with an exchangeability-robust corollary.} HCCP transfers the heteroscedastic head of DEGU from MPRA regression to binary variant classification with finite-sample local coverage, pairing a nonconformity score $s(x,y) = |y - \phat(x)| / \shat(x)$ with Mondrian-$(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ calibration. T3 gives exact bin-conditional coverage at rate $1/(n_{k,b}+1)$; T3$'$ (via \citet{barber2023exchangeability} Thm.~2 at the cell level) supplies a uniform finite-sample lower bound when our per-chromosome KS audit detects cell-level exchangeability violations on Mendelian.
% [De-AI] Original: ... HCCP is the only single-fold partition we evaluated --- across ... --- that holds ...
    \item \textbf{Empirical validation on TraitGym + ProteinGym + synthetic.} On TraitGym Mendelian / Complex at $\pi_{\min} = 0.10$ and the recommended operating point ($K_{\mathrm{eval}} = 3$ / $5$; per-cell minority $\geq 100$), HCCP is the only single-fold partition we evaluated (across split CP, $\shat$-Mondrian, class-Mondrian, RLCP, weighted CP, and SC-CP at matched $(\phat, \shat)$) that holds $\mathrm{cov}_{|Y=1} \geq 0.85$ and $\shat$-bin gap $\leq 0.20$ on both datasets ($B=200$ chrom-bootstrap CIs throughout). % [Polish] Original: The conformal gain is decoupled from base-predictor accuracy (App.~\ref{app:tables:hccp_logreg}). ProteinGym \citep{notin2023proteingym} cross-domain replication; synthetic $n$-sweep verifies the dimension-free $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate.
The conformal gain is decoupled from base-predictor accuracy (App.~\ref{app:tables:hccp_logreg}). We replicate on ProteinGym \citep{notin2023proteingym} for cross-domain validation, and a synthetic $n$-sweep verifies the dimension-free $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate.
\end{enumerate}
