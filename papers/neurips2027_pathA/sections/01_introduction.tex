Clinical variant interpretation drives consequential decisions \citep{richards2015acmg}, yet machine-learned variant effect predictors (VEPs) collapse evidence into a single probability \citep{kircher2014cadd,linder2025borzoi,benegas2023gpnmsa,benegas2025traitgym} without explicit confidence. Conformal prediction guarantees distribution-free coverage \citep{vovk2005algorithmic,vovk2003mondrian,romano2019cqr,barber2021jackknife,barber2023exchangeability}, yet \citet{barber2020limits} rule out pointwise conditional coverage in finite samples. VEP therefore needs a method that (i) consumes a learned heteroscedastic head $\shat(x)$ as in DEGU (Deep Ensemble with Gaussian Uncertainty) \citep{zhou2026degu}, (ii) stratifies calibration by both label and $\shat$-bin to recover as much conditioning as finite samples permit, and (iii) holds local coverage across distribution shifts. The gap binds for VEP: TraitGym minority prevalence sits at $\pi_{\min} \approx 0.10$, and chrom-LOO, trait-LOO, and cross-dataset shifts are intrinsic.

The technical question is: which Mondrian partition simultaneously delivers (i) class-conditional coverage under extreme imbalance and (ii) bin-local coverage under heteroscedasticity? Single-axis answers fail individually --- $\shat$-Mondrian \citep{bostrom2020mondrian} attains local coverage in shape but loses minority-class coverage ($\mathrm{cov}_{|Y=1} = 0.62$ on TraitGym Complex; the resulting minority-cell undercoverage drives its $\shat$-bin gap up to $0.83$); class-Mondrian \citep{sadinle2019lac} attains class coverage but leaves a bin-local gap $13.4\times$ wider than the joint partition on Complex (bootstrap mean, $B=200$). Within the equi-bin Mondrian-$K$ family, we prove the joint $(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ Mondrian attains both with worst-cell coverage gap $G(K^\star) \leq 2\sqrt{L_F R / (\pi_{\min} n)}$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:t5oracle}) and a matching $\Omega(\sqrt{L_F R / (\pi_{\min} n)})$ finite-sample lower bound (Theorem~\ref{thm:t5lower}); $\pi_{\min}$ enters \emph{explicitly} as a $\pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ constant.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{First finite-sample rate for joint class-conditional + heteroscedastic-local CP, with explicit $\pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ dependence and a within-class matching lower bound} (Theorems~\ref{thm:t5oracle}, \ref{thm:t5lower}). The $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate is dimension-free under a 1-D Lipschitz assumption on $\shat$. The lower bound is tight \emph{within the equi-bin Mondrian-$K$ family} (not a procedure-class minimax: SC-CP attains $O(n^{-2/3})$ on a different axis, App.~\ref{app:proofs}). To our knowledge this is the first bound in which $\pi_{\min}$ enters the rate as a $\pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ constant; concurrent imbalanced-CP work \citep{xie2025imbalancedcp,ding2023classcond,shi2024augmentedrank} provides marginal or class-conditional rates without the $\pi_{\min}$-local product.
    \item \textbf{HCCP, a post-hoc framework with an exchangeability-robust corollary.} HCCP extends DEGU's heteroscedastic head from MPRA regression to binary variant classification with finite-sample local coverage, pairing a nonconformity score $s(x,y) = |y - \phat(x)| / \shat(x)$ with Mondrian-$(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ calibration. T3 gives exact bin-conditional coverage at rate $1/(n_{k,b}+1)$; T3$'$ (via \citet{barber2023exchangeability} Thm.~2 at the cell level) supplies a uniform finite-sample certificate that is non-vacuous on Complex and \emph{loose by construction on Mendelian} (per-chromosome KS rejects A2-cell exchangeability at $46.2\%$, App.~\ref{app:data}); we do not claim T3$'$ binds the Mendelian bin-conditional gap empirically.
    \item \textbf{Empirical validation on TraitGym + ProteinGym + synthetic.} On TraitGym Complex ($n = 11{,}400$, $\pi_{+} = 0.10$), HCCP's worst-cell $\shat$-bin gap ($0.060 \pm 0.031$) is disjoint from all six matched-$(\phat, \shat)$ baselines we evaluated (vanilla split, $\shat$-Mondrian, class-Mondrian, RLCP, weighted CP, SC-CP; baseline gaps $\geq 0.805$); $B=200$ chrom-bootstrap CIs $[0.019, 0.119]$ vs.\ $[0.770, 0.879]$. On Mendelian ($n = 3{,}380$) HCCP attains $\mathrm{cov}_{|Y=1} = 0.875 \pm 0.040$ and gap $0.173 \pm 0.192$ at the recommended $K_{\mathrm{eval}} = 3$ (the only operating point with per-cell minority $\geq 100$); the $\pm 1\sigma$ band overlaps weighted CP, and a $K_{\mathrm{eval}}$ sweep shows the regime boundary at $K_{\mathrm{eval}} \geq 5$ \emph{predicted} by Eq.~\ref{eq:gap_decomp}'s bias-variance decomposition. The conformal-side gain is decoupled from base-predictor accuracy (App.~\ref{app:tables:hccp_logreg}); ProteinGym \citep{notin2023proteingym} cross-domain replication reports degraded-but-bounded coverage; a synthetic $n$-sweep verifies the dimension-free $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate (slope $-0.50 \pm 0.05$, App.~\ref{app:synthetic}).
\end{enumerate}
