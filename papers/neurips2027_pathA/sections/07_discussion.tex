\paragraph{Clinical implications.}
HCCP prediction sets are not a replacement for expert review; they are a calibrated abstention signal. A singleton set $\pset(x) = \{k\}$ is a confident call; a set containing both labels or neither is an explicit abstention. On TraitGym Mendelian, HCCP produces singletons for $\approx 78\%$ of variants at $\alpha = 0.10$ ($K_{\mathrm{eval}} = 5$, Tab.~\ref{tab:main}; cf.\ $0.827$ at $K_{\mathrm{eval}} = 3$ in Tab.~\ref{tab:cp_baselines}). Operating-point selection by miscoverage level is dataset-specific: $\alpha = 0.10$ for high-stakes rare-disease classification, $\alpha = 0.20$ for population-screening triage of complex traits where the abstention burden is otherwise prohibitive (cf.\ \citet{pcos2025conformal} report $41\%$ abstention at $\alpha \approx 0.20$ in a clinical screening setting); App.~\ref{app:alpha} reports the singleton-vs-abstention tradeoff across $\alpha \in [0.05, 0.50]$.

\paragraph{Operational envelope and limitations.}
The coverage guarantees of HCCP hold when (a) per-cell minority $\geq 100$, (b) base AUPRC $\gtrsim 0.30$, and (c) per-cell KS-reject $\lesssim 50\%$. \ref{ass:A1} (chrom-group exchangeability) is load-bearing for T3; T3$'$ absorbs A2-cell violations via the chrom-TV term on Mendelian ($46.2\%$ reject, $K$-invariant in $[40, 46]\%$ across $K_{\mathrm{eval}} \in \{2, 3, 5, 8, 10\}$; App.~\ref{app:data}); \ref{ass:ASL} is required only for T5. We report four labelled out-of-envelope regimes as case studies, not coverage claims: \textbf{(R1)} Mendelian $K_{\mathrm{eval}} \geq 5$ violates (a) at per-cell minority $< 70$ (§\ref{sec:experiments:h2h}; reversal \emph{anticipated} by Eq.~\eqref{eq:gap_decomp}'s variance-term scaling); \textbf{(R2)} the Skeletal/connective OMIM cluster ($n = 470$, $1/3$ Mendelian clusters) violates (a) with $\mathrm{cov}_{|Y=1} = 0.745$ (§\ref{sec:experiments:ood}); \textbf{(R3)} the M$\to$C cross-dataset direction jointly violates (b) (AUPRC $0.205$ vs.\ ID $0.353$) and (c) (ClinVar vs.\ GWAS catalog selection \citep{tibshirani2019weighted}),
with marginal coverage $0.738$ ($0.004$ below the Barber 2023 Thm.~2 proxy bound of $0.742$); the forward C$\to$M and ProteinGym cross-domain violate neither precondition and HCCP holds; \textbf{(R4)} ProteinGym $6/50$ outliers violate (b) (CD19 AUPRC $0.24$; App.~\ref{app:proteingym}). \textbf{Scope}: (i) marginal-score TV proxy underestimates swap-TV by $\approx 0.036$ on M$\to$C (App.~\ref{app:data}); (ii) oracle $K^\star$ is large-sample, the binding constraint at $n \leq 11{,}400$ is the per-cell floor $n_{k,b} \geq \lceil 1/\alpha \rceil$, modal nested-CV $\hat K \in \{5, 8\}$ Mendelian / $5$ Complex (App.~\ref{app:tables}); (iii) DEGU CNN end-to-end port is out of scope (200\,bp DNA windows); we ablate the $\shat$-mechanism only at matched GBM backbone; (iv) per-assay $\shat$ adaptation for R4 outliers remains future work.

\paragraph{Broader impact.}
VEP systems inform consequential clinical decisions (cascade testing, reproductive choices). HCCP provides calibrated abstention but does not guarantee clinical safety: the $\approx 78\%$ singletons on Mendelian still carry a $10\%$ finite-sample miscoverage floor, and abstention must be paired with expert review to translate coverage into clinical utility. Population-stratified deployment requires re-calibration on ancestry-conditional folds (a fourth Mondrian axis); TraitGym is built from ClinVar and the GWAS catalog and over-represents European-ancestry individuals. The ACMG/AMP consensus \citep{richards2015acmg} treats computational predictions as PP3/BP4 supporting evidence, not stand-alone classification; HCCP singletons should be integrated into multi-evidence frameworks, not deployed as diagnostic.
