We introduced HCCP, a post-hoc framework for variant effect prediction that pairs a learned heteroscedastic head $\shat$ with Mondrian-$(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ conformal calibration. Three contributions: (1) the joint product partition, the unique Mondrian refinement we evaluated that simultaneously satisfies class-conditional and bin-local coverage at $\pi_{\min} = 0.10$ on TraitGym; (2) a finite-sample $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate within the equi-bin Mondrian-$K$ class with matching lower bound and explicit $\pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ constant (T5.1, T5.2); (3) empirical validation on TraitGym Mendelian / Complex with cross-domain replication on ProteinGym and synthetic dimension-free rate verification. Natural extensions include end-to-end joint training of $(\phat, \shat)$, application to richer base predictors (AlphaGenome \citep{avsec2026alphagenome}), and population-stratified Mondrian for ancestry-conditional coverage.
