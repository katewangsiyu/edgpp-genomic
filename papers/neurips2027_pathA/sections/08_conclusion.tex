% Conclusion --- ~0.25 page

We introduced HCCP, a post-hoc framework for variant effect prediction that pairs a learned heteroscedastic head $\shat$ with Mondrian-$(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ conformal calibration. Three contributions: (1) to our knowledge, the first finite-sample $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate for joint class-conditional + heteroscedastic-local CP, with explicit $\pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ dependence and a within-class matching $\Omega(n^{-1/2})$ lower bound on the equi-bin Mondrian-$K$ family (Theorems~\ref{thm:t5oracle}, \ref{thm:t5lower}; not a procedure-class minimax: SC-CP attains $O(n^{-2/3})$ on a different axis); (2) the HCCP framework, with T3 exact bin-conditional coverage and a Barber-2023 robustness corollary T3$'$ that is non-vacuous on Complex but loose-by-construction on Mendelian (KS rejects A2-cell exchangeability at $46.2\%$); (3) empirical validation on TraitGym + Open Targets + ProteinGym + synthetic, decisively dominant on Complex (HCCP $\shat$-bin gap $0.060 \pm 0.031$ vs.\ $\geq 0.805$ across all six matched-$(\phat, \shat)$ baselines we evaluated; $B = 200$ chrom-bootstrap CIs disjoint) and on Open Targets matched-9 ($16.7\times$ at the recommended operating point on a variant- and feature-disjoint third dataset, App.~\ref{app:open_targets}), with a more contested signal on Mendelian (HCCP $\pm 1\sigma$ band overlaps weighted CP at $K_{\mathrm{eval}} = 3$, the only operating point with per-cell minority $\geq 100$; the regime boundary at $K_{\mathrm{eval}} \geq 5$ is consistent with Eq.~\ref{eq:gap_decomp}'s bias-variance scaling, not a model-class limitation). The conformal-side gain is decoupled from the choice of base predictor (HCCP-on-LogReg gives an essentially identical $\shat$-bin gap, App.~\ref{app:tables:hccp_logreg}); a synthetic $n$-sweep verifies the dimension-free $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate (slope $-0.44 \pm 0.08$ over 6 curves; T5.1 prediction $-0.50$ within $1$\,SD). Natural extensions: end-to-end joint training of $(\phat, \shat)$, richer base predictors (AlphaGenome \citep{avsec2026alphagenome}), and population-stratified Mondrian for ancestry-conditional coverage.
