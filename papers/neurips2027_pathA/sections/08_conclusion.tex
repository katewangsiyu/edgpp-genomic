% Conclusion --- ~0.25 page

We introduced HCCP, a post-hoc framework for variant effect prediction that pairs a learned heteroscedastic head $\shat$ with Mondrian-$(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ conformal calibration. Three contributions: (1) a finite-sample $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate within the equi-bin Mondrian-$K$ class with a within-class tight lower bound and explicit $\pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ constant (T5.1, T5.2; not a procedure-class minimax); (2) the HCCP framework, with T3 exact bin-conditional coverage and a Barber-2023 robustness corollary T3$'$ that supplies a non-vacuous (loose-by-construction) worst-case certificate when cell-level exchangeability is empirically rejected; (3) empirical validation on TraitGym Mendelian / Complex with $B = 200$ chromosome-level bootstrap CIs --- at the recommended operating point ($K_{\mathrm{eval}} = 3$ Mendelian, $K_{\mathrm{eval}} = 5$ Complex), HCCP is the only single-fold partition we evaluated, among normalized split, $\shat$-Mondrian, class-Mondrian, RLCP, weighted CP, and SC-CP, that simultaneously satisfies class-conditional and bin-local coverage at $\pi_{\min} = 0.10$. The conformal-side gain is decoupled from the choice of base predictor (HCCP-on-LogReg gives an essentially identical $\shat$-bin gap, App.~\ref{app:tables:hccp_logreg}), with cross-domain replication on ProteinGym and synthetic dimension-free rate verification. Natural extensions: end-to-end joint training of $(\phat, \shat)$, richer base predictors (AlphaGenome \citep{avsec2026alphagenome}), and population-stratified Mondrian for ancestry-conditional coverage.
