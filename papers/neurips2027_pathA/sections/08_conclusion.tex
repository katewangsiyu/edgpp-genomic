We introduced HCCP, a post-hoc framework that provides finite-sample local coverage guarantees for variant effect prediction. Three contributions are worth highlighting: (1) the Mondrian-$(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ partition, which simultaneously corrects class-conditional and heteroscedastic coverage imbalance (T3); (2) the adaptive bin-count selection with a dimension-free $O(n^{-1/2})$ local coverage rate and matching lower bound (T5); and (3) a three-axis empirical validation on TraitGym showing that $\shat$-bin coverage gaps stay below 0.04 across chrom-LOO, trait-LOO, and cross-dataset shifts.

Natural extensions include jointly training $\phat$ and $\shat$ end-to-end (our decoupled pipeline sacrifices expressiveness for modularity), applying HCCP to richer base predictors such as AlphaGenome \citep{avsec2026alphagenome}, and extending T5 to non-uniform bin constructions. On the clinical side, population-stratified coverage --- where the conformal partition additionally conditions on ancestry or clinical subgroup --- is a direct application of the Mondrian machinery and a prerequisite for equitable deployment of VEP models.
