\subsection{Setup}
\label{sec:formulation:setup}

Let $X \in \X \subseteq \mathbb{R}^d$ denote a variant feature vector, $Y \in \{0, 1\}$ its pathogenic label, and $C \in \mathcal{C} = \{1, \ldots, 22, X\}$ its chromosome. We observe $\D = \{(X_i, Y_i, C_i)\}_{i=1}^n$. TraitGym's chrom-LOO protocol fixes a test chromosome $c^\star$ and uses $\D^{(-c^\star)}$ for train + calibration.

\begin{assumption}[Chrom-group exchangeability]
\label{ass:A1}
Conditional on $C = c$, the samples $\{(X_i, Y_i) : C_i = c\}$ are exchangeable; no assumption is made across chromosomes.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Score stationarity across folds]
\label{ass:A2}
The trained score function $s(\cdot, \cdot; \phat, \shat)$ is invariant to the held-out chrom rotation within the training fold (proof-level detail; empirically verified via KS test on per-chrom score distributions, Day 13).
\end{assumption}

\subsection{Base aggregator and heteroscedastic head}
\label{sec:formulation:heads}

$\phat: \X \to [0, 1]$ is a gradient-boosted probabilistic classifier. $\shat: \X \to \mathbb{R}_+$ is a second regressor trained on chrom-LOO residuals $r_i = |Y_i - \phat^{(-C_i)}(X_i)|$ with Gaussian log-likelihood objective (Nix-Weigend / DEGU's hetero baseline).

\subsection{Nonconformity score and prediction set}
\label{sec:formulation:score}

We define the feature-dependent score
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:score}
s(x, y) := \frac{|y - \phat(x)|}{\shat(x) + \epsilon}, \qquad \epsilon = 10^{-6}.
\end{equation}

For each class $k \in \{0, 1\}$ and $\shat$-bin $b \in \{1, \ldots, K\}$, the Mondrian threshold is
\[
\qhat_{k,b} = \mathrm{Quantile}\bigl(S_{k,b}, \lceil (n_{k,b} + 1)(1 - \alpha) \rceil / n_{k,b}\bigr),
\]
where $S_{k,b}$ collects calibration scores in the $(k, b)$ cell. The prediction set is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:pset}
\pset(x) = \bigl\{k \in \{0, 1\} : s(x, k) \leq \qhat_{k, b(x)}\bigr\},
\end{equation}
with $b(x)$ the $\shat$-bin index of $x$ under the calibration-fold bin edges.
