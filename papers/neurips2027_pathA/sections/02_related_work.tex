\paragraph{Conformal prediction with conditioning.}
Split / Mondrian CP \citep{vovk2005algorithmic,vovk2003mondrian,angelopoulos2022gentle} provides marginal / group-conditional coverage; \citet{barber2020limits} rule out pointwise conditional coverage. Normalized scores with a learned $\shat$ originate with \citet{papadopoulos2008normalized} and \citet{lei2018distribution} for regression; \citet{bostrom2020mondrian} systematized Mondrian conformal regression with normalized scores; CQR \citep{romano2019cqr} is the regression analog of our score. Recent local-CP work targets feature-space coverage at slower rates: \textbf{RLCP} \citep{hore2025rlcp} achieves $O(n^{-2/(d+2)})$ kernel-localized coverage (intractable at $d \approx 7{,}700$); \textbf{Self-Calibrating CP} \citep{vanderlaan2024sccp} achieves $O(n^{-2/3})$ on the $\hat\mu$-axis via isotonic monotonicity \citep{deng2021isotonic}, on a different axis from ours. \citet{tibshirani2019weighted,gibbs2021adaptive,barber2023exchangeability} cover distribution-shift robustness; we use Barber 2023 Thm.~2 for our T3$'$. \citet{dewolf2025conditional} analyze conditional validity of heteroscedastic CP for regression; we extend to classification with $\pi_{\min}$ explicit. \citet{yao2026nonasymp} (concurrent) prove a dimension-free $O(n^{-1/2})$ efficiency rate for split-CP quantile regression trained via SGD; their result establishes the rate on linear quantile reg. We contribute orthogonal axes — the partition-family analysis (equi-bin Mondrian-$K$), explicit $\pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ constant, and within-class tight lower bound — not a faster rate. \citet{plassier2025cp2hpd} target approximate conditional validity via density-ratio reweighting under TV-distance; an alternative to discrete $\shat$-bin Mondrian without finite-sample bin-conditional guarantees. \citet{crps_binning2026} (concurrent) identifies optimal bin count under covariate-sorted contiguous binning as an open problem; Theorem~\ref{thm:t5oracle} resolves a special case for $(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ Mondrian under class imbalance.

\paragraph{Class-conditional and imbalanced CP.}
\citet{sadinle2019lac} introduced label-conditional CP (Mondrian-by-$y$); \citet{shi2024augmentedrank,ding2023classcond} adapt class-conditional CP for many classes / small per-class samples; \citet{xie2025imbalancedcp} (concurrent) study open-set + imbalanced classification with reweighting and obtain a $O(n^{-1/2})$ marginal-coverage bound \emph{without} $\pi_{\min}$-local product. \citet{bairaktari2025kandinsky} (concurrent) extend group-conditional CP to overlapping/fractional groups with minimax-optimal coverage subsuming class-conditional and Mondrian as static-group special cases; their framework requires pre-specified groups and does not cover our data-dependent $(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ partition or provide a matching lower bound. None of these address the heteroscedastic axis: in our H2H ablation (Tab.~\ref{tab:h2h}), single-axis Mondrian predictors face a fundamental tradeoff in the imbalanced regime — class-Mondrian preserves $\mathrm{cov}_{|Y=1}$ but loses bin-local coverage (gap $0.32$--$0.45$), while $\shat$-Mondrian preserves local coverage but loses $\mathrm{cov}_{|Y=1}$ down to $0.66$ at $\pi_{\min} = 0.10$. HCCP's joint $(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ Mondrian is, to our knowledge, the first construction simultaneously satisfying both objectives in this regime, with T5 quantifying the price as $O(\sqrt{L_F R / (\pi_{\min} n)})$.

\paragraph{Heteroscedastic UQ and VEP benchmarks.}
\citet{nix1994nll} originated Gaussian NLL for learned variance; \citet{lakshminarayanan2017simple} introduced deep ensembles. \textbf{DEGU} \citep{zhou2026degu} pioneered ensemble distillation to a Gaussian-uncertainty student for MPRA regression and motivated conformal prediction as future work; we extend its $\shat$ machinery to (a) variant classification and (b) finite-sample local coverage with adaptive partition granularity. We do not claim a head-to-head defeat of DEGU: DEGU is a regression pipeline on MPRA with a Keras CNN ensemble, whereas HCCP targets binary classification on TraitGym/ProteinGym; the ``DEGU-lite'' ablation in App.~\ref{app:tables} reimplements the ensemble-distilled $\shat$ at our matched GBM backbone solely to isolate the choice of variance source, not to replicate DEGU's published pipeline. \textbf{TraitGym} \citep{benegas2025traitgym} standardizes VEP evaluation; features include CADD \citep{kircher2014cadd}, Borzoi \citep{linder2025borzoi}, GPN-MSA \citep{benegas2023gpnmsa}; AlphaGenome \citep{avsec2026alphagenome} is a recent foundation model used in our App.~\ref{app:tables} ablation. CP applications in genomic medicine \citep{papangelou2025cpgenomics,wang2025cqrprs,pcos2025conformal} target drug response or polygenic risk scores in a regression / clinical-screening setting; HCCP is, to our knowledge, the first CP framework with formal class-conditional and bin-local coverage guarantees for binary variant pathogenicity.
