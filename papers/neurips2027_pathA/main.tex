\begin{abstract}
How fine should the Mondrian partition of a conformal predictor be under heteroscedasticity and class imbalance? The bin count $K$ governs whether prediction sets attain bin-local coverage --- the per-region calibration that pointwise conditioning provably cannot deliver in finite samples \citep{barber2020limits}. For binary conformal classification \emph{within the equi-bin Mondrian-$K$ family}, we prove a tight finite-sample $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate on the worst-cell coverage gap, $G(K^\star) \leq 2\sqrt{L_F R / (\pi_{\min} n)}$ at $K^\star = \lfloor\sqrt{L_F R \pi_{\min} n}\rfloor$, with a matching lower bound that places the minority-class prevalence $\pi_{\min}$ explicitly in the constant (Theorem~\ref{thm:t5oracle}, \ref{thm:t5lower}); the rate is dimension-free because conditioning is on the 1-D summary $\shat(x)$. The theorem instantiates as \textbf{HCCP} (Heteroscedastic Class-Conditional Conformal Prediction), a post-hoc framework pairing a learned variance head $\shat$ with Mondrian-$(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ calibration; an explicit robustness counterpart (T3$'$, via \citet{barber2023exchangeability}) delivers coverage under cell-level exchangeability violations, which our per-chromosome KS audit identifies as the binding regime on Mendelian. On TraitGym variant effect prediction \citep{benegas2025traitgym}, HCCP is the unique single-fold partition simultaneously satisfying minority-class and bin-local coverage on both Mendelian ($n = 3{,}380$) and Complex ($n = 11{,}400$), tightening the bin-local coverage gap by more than an order of magnitude over off-the-shelf normalized-Mondrian, kernel-localized, weighted, and self-calibrating CP at matched $(\phat, \shat)$, with cross-domain replication on ProteinGym \citep{notin2023proteingym}. The GBM aggregator on TraitGym features reaches AUPRC $0.900$ on Mendelian, a $+14.8$\,pp improvement over the published LogReg baseline.
\end{abstract}
