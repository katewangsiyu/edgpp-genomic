\begin{abstract}
How fine should the Mondrian partition of a conformal predictor be under heteroscedasticity and class imbalance? The bin count $K$ governs whether prediction sets attain bin-local coverage --- the per-region calibration that pointwise conditioning provably cannot deliver in finite samples \citep{barber2020limits}. For binary conformal classification \emph{within the equi-bin Mondrian-$K$ family}, we prove a tight finite-sample $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate on the worst-cell coverage gap, $G(K^\star) \leq 2\sqrt{L_F R / (\pi_{\min} n)}$ at $K^\star = \lfloor\sqrt{L_F R \pi_{\min} n}\rfloor$, with a matching finite-sample lower bound on the same family that exposes an explicit $\pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ constant (Theorem~\ref{thm:t5oracle}, \ref{thm:t5lower}); the rate is dimension-free because conditioning is on the 1-D summary $\shat(x)$. The result instantiates as \textbf{HCCP} (Heteroscedastic Class-Conditional Conformal Prediction), a post-hoc framework pairing a learned variance head $\shat$ with Mondrian-$(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ calibration. An explicit robustness corollary (T3$'$, via \citet{barber2023exchangeability}) certifies coverage under cell-level exchangeability violations identified by our per-chromosome KS audit on Mendelian. On TraitGym variant effect prediction \citep{benegas2025traitgym}, HCCP is the only single-fold partition we evaluated --- among normalized split, $\shat$-Mondrian, class-Mondrian, RLCP, weighted CP and SC-CP at matched $(\phat, \shat)$ --- that simultaneously satisfies $\mathrm{cov}_{|Y=1} \geq 0.85$ and $\shat$-bin gap $\leq 0.20$ on both Mendelian ($n = 3{,}380$) and Complex ($n = 11{,}400$), tightening the bin-local gap by $33\times$ on Complex and $2.3\times$ on Mendelian over the strongest single-axis Mondrian baseline (class-Mondrian), with cross-domain replication on ProteinGym \citep{notin2023proteingym}. A $K_{\mathrm{eval}}$ sensitivity sweep (App.~\ref{app:tables:keval_sensitivity}) shows HCCP's Complex advantage is robust across $K_{\mathrm{eval}} \in [2, 10]$ ($3$–$34\times$); on Mendelian weighted CP narrows HCCP's margin to $1.15\times$ at the recommended operating point and overtakes HCCP at high $K_{\mathrm{eval}}$ where the per-cell minority count drops below $\sim 70$. The GBM aggregator on TraitGym features reaches AUPRC $0.900$ on Mendelian, a $+14.8$\,pp improvement over the published LogReg baseline.
\end{abstract}
