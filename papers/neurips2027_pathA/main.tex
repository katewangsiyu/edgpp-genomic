\begin{abstract}
We prove the first dimension-free optimal-partition bound for Mondrian conformal classification under Lipschitz score heteroscedasticity, together with a matching minimax lower bound, and apply it to variant effect prediction (VEP). Our framework, \textbf{HCCP} (Heteroscedastic Class-Conditional Conformal Prediction), pairs a gradient-boosted aggregator with a learned variance head $\shat$ and a Mondrian conformal calibration stratified by $(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$. HCCP is the smallest modification of split conformal that simultaneously admits finite-sample marginal, class-conditional, and $\shat$-bin-local coverage. The central theoretical result (T5) shows the optimal bin count satisfies $K^\star = \lfloor\sqrt{L_F R \pi_{\min} n}\rfloor$ with worst-cell gap $G(K^\star) = O(n^{-1/2})$, dimension-free because conditioning is on the 1-D summary $\shat(x)$ rather than the full feature vector; a matching $\Omega(n^{-1/2})$ lower bound rules out faster rates. On TraitGym \citep{benegas2025traitgym}, HCCP matches or exceeds SOTA AUPRC --- 0.900 Mendelian / 0.353 Complex on the CADD+GPN-MSA+Borzoi feature set, rising to 0.912 Mendelian when AlphaGenome \citep{avsec2026alphagenome} tissue-specific predictions are added as a 140-dimensional feature panel --- with chromosome-level bootstrap 95\% CI on $\shat$-bin gap $[0.005, 0.054]$ (Mendelian) and $[0.002, 0.008]$ (Complex); the three-axis OOD stress test (chromosome-LOO, trait-LOO, cross-dataset) shows $\shat$-bin coverage gap $\leq 0.04$ across all regimes, reaching the finite-sample floor of $0.002$–$0.004$ under trait-LOO. Our ablations position HCCP as a principled strengthening of DEGU's heteroscedastic distillation \citep{zhou2026degu}: on Complex traits where aleatoric noise dominates, supervised $\shat$ achieves $4.3\times$ better worst-cell coverage than ensemble-variance $\shat$. A cross-domain stress test on ProteinGym \citep{notin2023proteingym} --- 50 held-out protein assays with features engineered de novo from mutation physicochemistry --- shows HCCP's marginal coverage tracks the $0.90$ target to within $0.013$ on aggregate ($0.887 \pm 0.028$) and $44/50$ assays fall within the $\pm 0.05$ band, evidence that the coverage machinery is a property of the conformal calibration rather than of TraitGym.
\end{abstract}
