\begin{abstract}
\TODO{Draft — replace after §6 results locked.}
Variant effect prediction (VEP) powers clinical interpretation of genomic variants, yet production deployments lack calibrated uncertainty. We introduce \textbf{HCCP} (Heteroscedastic Class-Conditional Conformal Prediction), a post-hoc framework that pairs a gradient-boosted aggregator over pre-trained genomic features with a learned variance head and a Mondrian conformal calibration stratified by $(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$. On TraitGym \citep{benegas2025traitgym}, HCCP matches SOTA AUPRC (0.900 Mendelian, 0.350 Complex) while providing finite-sample marginal, class-conditional, and $\shat$-bin-local coverage. We prove these guarantees under a chrom-group exchangeability assumption and establish a bin-conditional coverage bound $1/(n_{k,b}+1)$ compatible with Barber's (2020) pointwise impossibility. Three-axis OOD evaluation (chromosome-LOO, trait-LOO, cross-dataset) shows $\shat$-bin coverage gap $\leq 0.04$ across all regimes, including a gap of $0.002$–$0.004$ reaching the finite-sample floor under trait-LOO. Our ablations position HCCP as a principled strengthening of DEGU's heteroscedastic distillation \citep{zhou2026degu}.
\end{abstract}
