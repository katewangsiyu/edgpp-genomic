\begin{abstract}
How fine should the Mondrian partition of a conformal predictor be when the underlying nonconformity score is heteroscedastic? Practitioners select this bin count $K$ ad hoc, yet $K$ governs whether downstream prediction sets attain bin-local coverage --- the per-region calibration that distribution-free uncertainty quantification is sold on, and that pointwise conditioning provably cannot deliver in finite samples \citep{barber2020limits}. We resolve this for binary conformal classification under a Lipschitz score-CDF assumption: the optimal Mondrian bin count is $K^\star = \lfloor\sqrt{L_F R \pi_{\min} n}\rfloor$, the worst-cell coverage gap satisfies $G(K^\star) = O(n^{-1/2})$, and a matching minimax lower bound $\Omega(n^{-1/2})$ rules out faster rates (Theorem~\ref{thm:t5oracle}, \ref{thm:t5lower}); the rate is dimension-free because conditioning is on the 1-D summary $\shat(x)$, not the full feature vector. The theorem instantiates as \textbf{HCCP} (Heteroscedastic Class-Conditional Conformal Prediction), a post-hoc framework pairing a learned variance head $\shat$ with Mondrian-$(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ calibration, which delivers marginal, class-conditional, and bin-local coverage from a single calibration fold. On TraitGym variant effect prediction \citep{benegas2025traitgym}, HCCP attains the predicted finite-sample floor $0.002$--$0.004$ on the bin-local coverage gap under trait-LOO and AUPRC $0.900$ on Mendelian (a $+14.8$\,pp improvement over the published baseline), with cross-domain replication on ProteinGym \citep{notin2023proteingym} reported in App.~\ref{app:proteingym}.
\end{abstract}
