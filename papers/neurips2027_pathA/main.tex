\begin{abstract}
We introduce \textbf{HCCP} (Heteroscedastic Class-Conditional Conformal Prediction): a post-hoc framework pairing a learned variance head $\shat(x)$ with Mondrian-$(y \times \shat\text{-bin})$ calibration via a feature-dependent nonconformity score. For binary conformal classification under joint class imbalance and heteroscedasticity, existing variants trade class-conditional coverage against bin-local coverage (a Pareto wall sharpened by \citet{barber2020limits}'s finite-sample pointwise impossibility); the gap binds for variant effect prediction (VEP), where TraitGym minority prevalence sits at $\pi_{\min} \approx 0.10$. Within the equi-bin Mondrian-$K$ family we prove the first finite-sample $O(n^{-1/2})$ rate for joint class-conditional + heteroscedastic-local CP with explicit $\pi_{\min}^{-1/2}$ dependence in the constant, matched \emph{within this family} by a $\Omega(n^{-1/2})$ within-class lower bound (Theorems~\ref{thm:t5oracle}, \ref{thm:t5lower}; not a procedure-class minimax); the bound is dimension-free given $\shat$ is 1-D Lipschitz. On TraitGym \citep{benegas2025traitgym} HCCP is decisively dominant on Complex ($n = 11{,}400$): the worst-cell $\shat$-bin gap is $0.060 \pm 0.031$ versus $\geq 0.805$ for all six matched-$(\phat, \shat)$ baselines we evaluated (vanilla split, $\shat$-Mondrian \citep{bostrom2020mondrian}, class-Mondrian, RLCP, weighted CP, SC-CP) --- $B = 200$ chrom-bootstrap CIs are disjoint. On Mendelian ($n = 3{,}380$) HCCP holds $\mathrm{cov}_{|Y=1} \geq 0.85$ and $\shat$-bin gap $\leq 0.20$ at the recommended operating point ($K_{\mathrm{eval}} = 3$; per-cell minority $\geq 100$) but its $\pm 1\sigma$ band overlaps weighted CP's; a $K_{\mathrm{eval}} \in [2,10]$ sweep shows the boundary at $K_{\mathrm{eval}} \geq 5$ where the per-cell variance term overtakes the Lipschitz-bias gain --- a regime change \emph{predicted} by our bias-variance decomposition (Eq.~\ref{eq:gap_decomp}). Cross-domain replication on ProteinGym \citep{notin2023proteingym} reports degraded but bounded coverage at OOD assays (App.~\ref{app:proteingym}).
\end{abstract}
